FINAL PROJECT (PROJECT DUE 12/06, PUBLIC PLAYTEST 12/13)

Working in groups of 6, you will prototype and complete a small, self-directed 3D game, or game level, in Unity. Details:

  • The emphasis should be on producing a well-designed and compelling game that is supported by its media.
  • The type of game is your choice, as long as it remains relevant to the material covered in this class, is small in scope, and is represents the shared input of the team.
  • Your team is responsible for determining team roles, delegating tasks each week, and meeting deadlines.
  • Each game should include a start screen, instructions, game scene, and ending scene (at minimum).

PROJECT DELIVERABLES:

  1. Completed, tested game builds for PC & Mac, plus all project files uploaded to class Drive of each team member (12/06)
  2. 2 updated builds running on your own computers with necessary controllers for public playtesting event (12/13)
  3. Documentation: 3 in-game screen shots, 1 short trailer no longer than a minute in length (12/13)

FINAL PROJECT: BRAINSTORMING, RESEARCH, & CONCEPTUALIZING

At 1:30 next week, each group will have 15 minutes to present their game idea. This should include:

  • A working title
  • Clear description of the game's concept, theme, and goals.
  • Describe your game's core mechanics through the use of compelling verbs. (Verbs? Good verbs?)
  • Core mechanics: Show two precedents which are successful & relevant to your game.
  • Concept Art & Sketches: Show two examples of concept art which are representative of your game's visuals. Also include two reference/inspirational images.
  • UI: Show two precedents for your game's UI using successful examples from similar games.
  • Clarification of each team member's expected roles.
