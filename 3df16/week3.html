HOMEWORK

3D SELF STUDY

Spend some time with the 3D modeling software of your choice. Experiment with...

  • Exporting an FBX or OBJ and importing it into Unity.
  • Working with the native filetype supported by your 3D modeling software.
  • Applying a material in your modeling software and manipulating it in Unity.
  • Applying a simple texture (or textures) in your modeling software.

Unity has thorough documentation to supporting importing assets from all major 3D modeling programs (link). Use it!

Alternative: If you are not comfortable with a 3D modeling program, begin researching options for sourcing models, find at least one model (you may need $), and bring it into Unity.

TurboSquid, OpenGameArt, and Unity's Asset Store are a few options, but there are others that might be worth investigating.

COURSE (DUE NEXT WEEK)

Working with a combination textured primitives and imported 3D assets, create a fun & challenging single level obstacle course for the ball rolling mechanic we worked on in class today.

Technical requirements:

  • Sketches -> greybox -> playable prototype.
  • Mix of in-engine primitives plus one imported 3D asset (minimum). Untextured, or minimally textured.
  • All complex imported assets must be greyboxed with primitives first.
  • These primitives must be left in the level for me to review (Put them in an empty GameObject and deactivate them.)
  • Level must have a goal.
  • Level must have conflict/challenging component (This doesn't mean punishing!)
  • Use of one UI element

Some possible inspiration:

  • Mini Golf and Croquet
  • Pinball or Bumper pool
  • Marble Madness (1984)
  • Super Monkey Ball(2001)
  • Odama (2006)
  • Rock of Ages (2011)

Upload project + build to class Drive folder by the start of class. We'll play these together at the start of next class.

Good Luck!
