A LITTLE BIT ON LEVEL DESIGN

Some characteristics of well-designed levels...

  • Are built around player mechanics & dynamics
  • Keep players motivated & engaged
  • Offer choices that inspire experimentation and reward the player
  • Direct the player's movement towards their goal(s)
  • Considers volume, not just 2-dimensional, space

Well-designed competitive, multiplayer levels also...

  • Create interesting, dynamic tension between players

TENSION

Managing tension in a multiplayer level is incredibly important

  • Makes the game feel exciting, by giving it a sense of urgency & unpredictability
  • Tension can be created by manipulating the relationships between things in the space
  • Remember, Backgammon...

4 Formal elements of Level Design

Composition

  • Relationship of visual elements in a scene
  • How the scene is framed from the player's point of view
  • Provides navigational/wayfinding aid to a player
  • Establishes emotional connection with a space
  • Link discussing the role of composition in Level Design here
  • See: Half-Life, Bioshock, Uncharted, Halo 2 Relic map

Shape Language and Color

  • Harmonious relationships can help an environment recede into the background
  • Dissonant relationships can help players easily parse interactive/important details from the environment.
  • See: Portal, Mirror's Edge, Dark Souls

Variation

  • Players respond to novelty and stimuli
  • Variation can be used to manipulate the pacing of the game (choke points)
  • Unexpected & surprising elements can be used to motivate players
  • Creating unpredictable paths are a good way of encouraging replayability & rewarding high level play
  • See: DOOM, DOOM (2016)

Symmetry

  • Uneven of asymmetrical elements can be used to destabilize a player or increase tension in a space
  • Mirrored levels are easier to balance
  • See: Counter Strike: Dust map, Splatoon, Unreal Tournament: Facing Worlds CTF map

MIDTERM PROJECT PITCHING AT 1:30

Each group will have roughly 10 minutes to present their game idea. This should include:

  1. Clear description of the game's concept and theme.
  2. Description of the game's systems, including goals, conflict, and core mechanics.
  3. Demonstration of two core mechanics which you've prototyped
  4. Preliminary sketches and first pass at a greyboxed level
  5. Sketches and concept art, with emphasis on how they support the concept
  6. Clarification of each team member's expected roles
